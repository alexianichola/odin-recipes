Description

This week is marathon week, so I’m focusing on foods made with wholesome ingredients that provide long-lasting energy to carry me through a full half marathon. One of my favorites is this Banana Chocolate Chip Muffin! I’ve been enjoying one before and after my long runs during training, and they’ve been the perfect energy boost.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup butter melted
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup mashed banana about 2-3 bananas
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Mash bananas in a small bowl until smooth.
  3. Mix wet ingredients – In a large bowl, combine melted butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, 1 cup mashed banana, eggs, Greek yogurt, and vanilla extract. Stir until well blended.
  4. Combine dry ingredients – In another bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Sift and mix – Sift the dry mixture into the wet ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Add chocolate chips – Gently fold in chocolate chips until evenly distributed.
  7. Prepare muffin tin – Line only the wells you’re using with parchment liners. Fill each lined well with about ¼ cup batter. For unused wells, leave unlined and fill halfway with water.
  8. Top muffins – Add a few extra chocolate chips and a sprinkle of flaky salt to each muffin.
  9. Bake – Bake for 10 minutes at 425°F (220°C). Without opening the oven, reduce temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for another 15–20 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean.
